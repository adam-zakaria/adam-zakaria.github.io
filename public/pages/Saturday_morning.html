I talked to Dan for two hours Thursday night. That was awesome. We met in Chicago after not talking for years, but being pretty tight in high school. I felt like I reconnected with my friend and rediscovered a part of my self I'd lost or otherwise forgotten. Just the night before I went out to trivia with a group of friends and was struck by how fast my friend's friend (my friend) was speaking. Sometimes I get worried that smart people talk fast. My girlfriend once told me I talk slowly. I think media sometimes depicts smart people as talking fast. I've worried that my cognitive processing is slow because I like to talk slowly. Dan talks slowly. And I think that's how ideas spread out and breathe. Dan is a math PhD at UChicago. We had long silences and surprisingly candid conversation, and I'm slightly embarrassed to say he broached the most taboo topics. It was pretty relaxing. It was also refreshing to hear a relatively unconcerned perspective. Dan is focused on his research and not much else. He hasn't thought about buying a house, I asked him. Some of my friends are ripping real estate right now. I guess sometimes I hope for some urgency or motivation - there's a flavor of compulsion that is palatable to me, I know it's out there.
